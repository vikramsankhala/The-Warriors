Krav Maga · Equalizer · Zero Strength

Low Kick to Groin

Krav Maga, Systema — the most reliable equalizer

A low, fast kick to the groin requires almost no force to incapacitate. The groin has no muscle protection. Even a child's kick causes involuntary doubling-over. Use your hip to drive the knee and leg — not leg strength alone.

Effort
Minimal
The groin is densely innervated. Impact triggers reflexive muscle contraction and pain response regardless of attacker size. A 90° knee drive from the hip multiplies force through leverage.
  • 1Standing: Drive knee straight upward — hip drives the motion. Target: between the legs.
  • 2If grabbed from front: Cup hands together, swing from low to high like an underhand pitch — both hands strike simultaneously.
  • 3If grabbed from behind: Reach back, grab and twist. Or elbow to solar plexus first, then drop and turn.
  • 4The moment they react: run. Do not stay.

Krav Maga · Muay Thai · Close Range

Knee Strike to Crotch

Krav Maga, Muay Thai — most powerful groin strike when close

When the attacker is right in front of you — grabbing you, leaning in, or within arm's reach — drive your knee straight up into their groin. The knee delivers more force than a kick from distance because your whole body weight drives through one point. Requires zero leg strength — the hip does all the work.

Effort
Minimal
The knee is the hardest bone structure in the leg. Driving upward uses the large hip flexors — no calf or quad strength needed. Impact concentrates your body weight through a small area. Works for women, children, and anyone regardless of size.
  • 1Range: Use when they're close — within 1–2 feet. Grabbing you, or approaching to grab.
  • 2Drive the knee up: Raise your knee straight upward — like marching in place, but sharp and fast. Hip flexors power the motion, not your leg.
  • 3Target: Between the legs. Aim for the center. A slight forward lean of your torso adds reach.
  • 4If grabbed from front: You're already close. One knee strike, then push off and run.
  • 5The moment they double over: run. Do not stay to strike again.

07
Krav Maga · Close Range · Stun

Elbow to Nose

Krav Maga, Systema — close-quarters equalizer

At close range, drive your elbow horizontally into the attacker's nose. The nose is cartilage — it breaks easily and causes tears, disorientation, and pain. Hip rotation provides all the power. Works for anyone, any size.

Effort
Very Low
The elbow is the hardest bony prominence on your body. The nose has no muscle or fat protection. Impact causes reflexive tearing (obscures vision), pain, and often bleeding — creating your escape window.
  • 1Forward elbow: Bend arm ~90°, drive elbow horizontally into nose — like closing a door with your elbow.
  • 2Backward elbow: If grabbed from behind, drive elbow sharply back into their face. Hip rotation adds power.
  • 3Rotate hip toward target — arm follows. No wind-up. Surprise = power.
  • 4Immediately create distance and run.

08
Aikido · Zen · No Fist

Palm Heel Strike

Aikido, traditional martial arts — safest strike for small hands

Why palm, not fist? A fist can break your own hand. The palm heel is the most natural striking surface — no technique required. Bend fingers back, expose the heel, step INTO the strike. Your body weight does the work.

Effort
Minimal
Even a 100 lb person stepping into a palm strike creates significant force. Target: nose or chin. Drive through — imagine pushing your palm through the back of their head.
  • 1Bend fingers back, expose heel of palm. Arm relaxed until impact.
  • 2Target: face (nose or chin). Step INTO the strike — body weight transfers.
  • 3Do not stop at the face — push through. Then create distance immediately.

10
Close Range · Square Facing · Universal

Instep Stomp (from Front)

Judo, Krav Maga — up close, square facing the attacker

When up close and square facing the attacker: crush their foot with your heel. Not short blows — full follow-through. Drive your body weight down through the heel into their instep and into the ground, like crushing a cockroach. The instep has 26 small bones and almost no muscle. Your full weight crushing through = enormous pressure.

Effort
Very Low
Up close, square facing. The stomp is a crush — full follow-through, not short blows. Power from your weight driving the heel down through their foot into the ground. Think: crush a cockroach on the floor.
  • 1Up close and square facing the attacker.
  • 2Raise foot, drive heel down onto top of their foot. Full follow-through — crush it into the ground with your body weight, like crushing a cockroach. Not short blows. Repeat if needed.
  • 3As they react: create distance, run.

11
Arnis · Seniors · Zero Grip Strength

Cane or Umbrella Defense

Filipino Arnis/Eskrima — for seniors who already carry a cane

A walking cane or sturdy umbrella provides reach without needing to be close. Grip with both hands comfortably spaced. Turn your body sideways and thrust the stick end point into the attacker's face — power comes mainly from the waist twist moving through the shoulder. Use full follow-throughs, not short blows. Keep moving in with repeated thrusts. Never step back. If the opponent catches the cane, use your elbow for sideways thrusts to the face — power emanates from the shoulder with repeated waist twists and full follow-throughs.

Effort
Minimal
Full follow-throughs — never short blows. Cane thrusts and elbow thrusts both. Keep moving in. Never step back. Legal everywhere.
  • 1Grip: Both hands on the cane or umbrella, comfortably spaced.
  • 2Body sideways: Turn your body sideways to the attacker.
  • 3Waist twist → shoulder: Thrust the stick end point into the face with full follow-throughs — not short blows. Keep moving in with repeated thrusts. Never step back.
  • 4Cane caught: If the opponent catches the cane, use your elbow for sideways thrusts to the face. Power emanates from the shoulder — repeated waist twists and full follow-throughs. Keep moving in.

◆ Scenarios ◆

Being Followed

Cross street, enter business, call someone loudly. If grabbed: palm strike, knee to groin, run.

Grabbed from Behind

Drop weight, elbow back, head back. Spin and run when grip loosens.

Home Intrusion

Lock door, call 911. Do not investigate. Position in corner, aim at door if armed.
